Working on my own robot sketch made me realize how different human‑driven design feels compared to something generated by AI. My version was built around a clear purpose: creating a small, friendly character for a simple game. Every choice I made—like putting the robot at the bottom of the screen, giving it smooth movement, and adding a smile‑to‑frown reaction—was intentional and tied to how I wanted the player to experience the game. It’s pretty straightforward and readable, and it reflects the way I think about interaction, personality, and gameplay.
The AI‑generated sketch, on the other hand, feels more like a visual experiment than a character with a role. It leans into dramatic lighting, noise‑based motion, and glitchy effects that give it a darker, more chaotic vibe. It’s cool in its own way, but it doesn’t have the same sense of purpose or emotional direction. Comparing the two made it clear that human creativity tends to be guided by intention and context, while AI creativity is more about remixing patterns and generating interesting visuals. It was a good reminder that AI can spark ideas, but the meaning and direction still come from the person behind the project.
